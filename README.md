## What This Is

This repository supports the long-form article **"The First Diagram Is Usually a Liar"** — a thought leadership piece exploring how diagrams, words, and AI-assisted synthesis interact in the early stages of systems thinking.

It is not a software application. It is a working archive: drafts, diagrams, slide decks, poll materials, and derivative artifacts built and iterated in public.

## Central Thesis

The first diagram you draw is usually premature. It reflects your assumptions, not the system.

ROY — Return on Your Words — is the measure of how much understanding a piece of communication actually produces. A diagram that clarifies a system for someone who could not follow the words is worth more than the words that prompted it into existence. But that diagram almost never arrives on the first try.

This project documents how to get to the diagram that earns its place.

## Why It Has Its Own Repo

Because the article has moving parts: Mermaid source, diagram contest rounds, a Council of AIs comparison, LinkedIn poll sequences, a GitHub Pages hub, Ko-fi companion materials, and multiple revision milestones tracked publicly.

A single document in a notes app does not cut it. This repo is the record.

## Publication Model

This project lives across several surfaces:

- **GitHub** — source of truth for drafts, diagrams, and version history
- **LinkedIn** — article publication, diagram contest polls, engagement series
- **GitHub Pages** (via `/web/`) — public-facing hub with links to all materials
- **Ko-fi** (via `/kofi/`) — premium companion materials for supporters

Each surface draws from this repo. Nothing gets published without a corresponding artifact here.

## Iteration Model

This is a public build, not a finished product drop.

- `v0.1` is the protoform — thesis established, argument rough
- `v0.2` through `v0.9` incorporate diagram contest rounds, poll outcomes, and council synthesis
- `v1.0` is the final article, ready for permanent publication

The repo preserves that lineage. The changelog lives in `/archive/changelog.md`.
