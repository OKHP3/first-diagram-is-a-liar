# Council of AIs

This folder contains materials from the Council of AIs comparison process used in the development of **"The First Diagram Is Usually a Liar."**

## What the Council Is

The Council of AIs is a structured approach to using multiple AI models — independently and in sequence — to generate, critique, refine, and synthesize diagrams and arguments.

Rather than using one AI as an oracle, each model is treated as a distinct voice with different strengths, biases, and blind spots. The council process surfaces disagreement, identifies convergence, and produces a synthesis that no single model would generate on its own.

## Files

- `interviews.md` — Prompts, responses, and raw outputs from each AI model
- `rankings.md` — Comparative rankings of diagrams and arguments by council members
- `scorecards.md` — Structured evaluation grids: clarity, accuracy, ROY, visual logic

## Why This Matters

The council process is part of the article's argument, not just a production method. It demonstrates that the first AI output is also usually a liar — and that iteration and comparison are as necessary for AI-generated diagrams as for human-drawn ones.
